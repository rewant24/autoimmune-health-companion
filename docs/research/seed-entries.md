## Patterns this dataset exposes (for visualization prototyping)

1. **Dysfunction vs. stiffness** — they co-move: flare day shows 90-min stiffness at 8/10 dysfunction; baseline 20–30 min at 3–4/10.
2. **Mood lag + lead** — mood drops *before* dysfunction peaks (day 6 mood 4, day 7 mood 3, day 8 dysfunction peaks) — suggests mood is a leading indicator.
3. **Sleep ↔ next-day function** — poor sleep (days 6–7, <6h) precedes flare. Steroid insomnia (days 10–12) shows as drop even while dysfunction improves.
4. **Allergen trigger → flare lag** — quinoa cross-contamination day 6 → full flare day 8 (≈48h window).
5. **Medication change impact window** — prednisone started day 10 (dysfunction 7), visible improvement by day 11 (5), near-baseline by day 13 (3) — 3-day impact.
6. **Humira dose unchanged across the flare** — the escalation was burst-steroid, not biologic change. This is realistic.
7. **Blood markers lag symptoms** — CRP/ESR still elevated day 16 even though dysfunction back to 3/10 since day 13.
